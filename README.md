# Liam Andell Portfolio

This portfolio was created to showcase my background in instructional design, training delivery, software development, and technical communication. It brings together my experience as a Training Specialist, AP Computer Science teacher, and developer into one place where employers can view my work, understand my process, and see how I approach building learning experiences and technical projects.
## 🌍 Live Portfolio

👉 https://liamandell.github.io/portfolio/

## About This Portfolio

I built this portfolio from the ground up using React, JavaScript, and TypeScript to create a clean, responsive, and user focused website. My goal was to design a site that not only presents my experience clearly, but also reflects the way I think about communication, usability, and structure. As someone who works at the intersection of learning, technology, and design, I wanted this portfolio to show both my instructional background and my technical foundation.

This project highlights my work in instructional design, training delivery, and software development. It includes selected materials from the Online EBT project for the state of Texas, along with GitHub projects that demonstrate my backend and frontend development experience.

## Why I Built It

I wanted a space that could go beyond a traditional resume and give employers a fuller picture of my work. My background is unique in that it blends education, adult learning, software systems, and visual communication. A portfolio gave me the opportunity to present that story in a more meaningful way.

I also wanted to demonstrate that I can build and maintain a website myself. Creating this portfolio allowed me to apply the same principles I use in training and instructional design to web development by thinking about the user experience, organizing information clearly, and making content easy to navigate.

## Background and Approach

My path into instructional design and training came through education and technology. As an AP Computer Science teacher, I created lessons and curriculum from the ground up, taught software development concepts, and learned how to make complex material approachable for different learners. That experience became the foundation for my work with adult learners.

In my role as a Training Specialist, I supported the Online EBT project for the state of Texas by helping create learning materials and training clinic staff to navigate Microsoft based and CRM systems. This work strengthened my ability to translate technical workflows into practical learning experiences that support user adoption and performance.

At the same time, I continued developing my technical skill set through software development. My full stack foundation was strengthened through coursework at Rice University, where I completed a software development certificate and gained experience with both frontend and backend technologies.

## Development Process

The process of creating this portfolio started with defining the story I wanted it to tell. I mapped out the main sections based on the areas I wanted employers to understand most clearly: instructional design, training delivery, software development, and featured work.

From there, I built the site structure in React and used reusable data driven sections for highlights, work samples, skills, and project cards. I focused on making the layout clean and readable, while also giving it enough visual polish to feel modern and professional.

As I developed the portfolio, I refined the content to better reflect my voice and professional background. I wanted the site to feel personal and relatable while still communicating my value clearly to employers in training, instructional design, learning and development, and technical roles.

## What This Portfolio Represents

This portfolio represents more than just a collection of projects. It reflects how I think, how I communicate, and how I solve problems. Whether I am creating a learning experience, building technical documentation, teaching software development, or designing a website, my goal is always the same: make something complex feel clear, useful, and approachable.
